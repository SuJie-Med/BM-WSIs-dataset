## Vision-Guided Textual Conditioning
   Vision-Guided Textual Conditioning (VGTC) first generates a low-resolution representation of the WSI through pyramid-based downsampling, as shown in Fig.1(a). The Visual Prior-Guided Localization (VPL) algorithm is then applied to identify bone marrow particle regions in the downsampled image, as illustrated in Fig.1(b). The localized regions are projected back onto the corresponding low-resolution image and combined with a fixed prompt template, ***“Please use a concise and clear sentence to describe the bone marrow particle region inside the red bounding box.”*** The image-text pairs are subsequently fed into a large language model (LLM) to generate region-specific textual descriptions, as shown in Fig.1(c).

## Vision-Language Guided ROI Anchor Region Localization
   We introduce CLIP to align image-region features with LLM-generated descriptions in a shared semantic embedding space. The image-text similarity is calculated to quantify the correspondence between visual regions and semantic concepts. Based on the CLIP visual encoder, gScoreCAM is further employed to generate semantic response maps by integrating gradient sensitivity and channel activation intensity, as shown in Fig.2(a). Subsequently, we propose Adaptive-Region-Aware Prompting (ARA-Prompt) to adaptively generate point prompts for segmentation according to the spatial distribution of semantic response regions, as illustrated in Fig.2(b). Finally, the semantic-aware point prompts generated by ARA-Prompt are fed into SAM-Med2D, enabling precise segmentation of low-resolution WSIs with vision-language priors and producing ROI anchor region masks, as shown in Fig.2(c).

## Boundary-aware Active ROI Localization Module
   Based on the generated ROI anchor regions, we further propose the Boundary-aware Active ROI Localization Module, which incorporates an Adaptive Multi-Ring Staggered Sampling Strategy (AMRSS) to construct an efficient retrieval space for LLM-driven diagnostic assessment. AMRSS progressively expands the binary ROI anchor mask through morphological operations to generate multi-scale ring-shaped candidate regions around the anchor boundaries. This design enables pathology-guided retrieval of clinically relevant peripheral regions. Candidate localization points are initially generated in the low-resolution thumbnail space and then mapped back to the original WSI coordinate system through scale transformation. A Poisson-disk sampling strategy is subsequently applied to constrain spatial distribution, reducing redundant sampling while improving regional coverage. Based on the retained localization points, high-resolution patches are extracted from the original WSI and evaluated by an LLM-based pathology-guided patch quality assessment framework. A fixed clinical prompt guides the LLM to categorize each patch into five diagnostic quality levels according to contamination ratio, cellular distribution, and spatial characteristics, and to determine its suitability for diagnostic use. Finally, high-quality patches that satisfy clinical requirements and their corresponding spatial coordinates are retained as reliable inputs for downstream cell-level detection, classification, and counting, as shown in Fig.3.
